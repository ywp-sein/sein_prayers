# Teach Us How to Commune with the Father as You Did

Recently I got a concordance to the Bible just by initiating a question, and God brought more fruits from that. Today, while I was queuing for food, the sister next to me shared that some words carry deeper meanings. For example, “life” in certain contexts does not necessarily refer to length of days, but to abundance.



This is the 48th weekly prayer:



Dear Heavenly Father,



Thank You for the abundance You are willing to give us. So far, I am already amazed by the journey You have brought me through. And the incredible thing is that this abundance will become immeasurably greater. All things not only work together for good for those who love You, but also form a path of learning for us to become more and more like You, and to see more of Your redemptive plan in our lives.



Dear Lord, thank You for prompting me today, through the preacher, to reflect again on the power of Your resurrection. I ask You to bless Your children in this city, that they may think more deeply about Your resurrection and how it can bring forth abundant life in them.



Thank You for the many things we can learn from You. But today, I ask one specific thing for Your children: teach us how to commune with the Father as You did, and help us know how wonderful it is that You taught us to pray beginning with “Our Father.” Thank You for the sacrifice that allows us to come boldly before the Father and pray: Hallowed be Thy name. May Your will be done.



Please teach us how to pray, so that we may know how to respond to Your love for us.



May the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Ghost, be with the one who loves Him. 



Amen.
